The Challenge

Represent time using a sensor and a moving part.

The brief for assessment 2 of Creative Prototyping was deliberately open: represent time using a sensor and some kind of moving part. The result had to be functional, physical, and meaningful.

If I imagined time as a material, I would say it's closest to water.

Concept

Time is violent under pressure, evaporates when you aren't looking, slows to a trickle when you are bored, and you'll drown in it if you're not careful.

— Reuben Sims, project notes

The whirlpool idea

The rough plan was a dish of water that responds to an observer — slowing and speeding up, becoming tumultuous or tepid depending on whether someone is watching. A whirlpool felt right: it has calm and chaos depending on the angle. It represents the "calm dread" of watching time flow around you, pushing you through deadlines, life events, and birthdays like some sort of demonic lazy river.

Sensor

Motion detector — detects the presence and proximity of an observer.

Moving Part

Magnetic stirrer motor — creates a whirlpool in the water vessel above.

How it was built

After exploring ripples, waves, and jets, the whirlpool proved most compelling. A magnetic chemistry stirrer from Amazon ($20) was disassembled — inside was a motorised magnet that creates a rotating field. A magnetic pill (stirrer bar) inside the water vessel spins to match it, creating the whirlpool effect.

The housing was modelled quickly in Fusion 360 — rough but functional — and rendered in Blender for presentation. The Arduino motion sensor adjusts the stirrer speed in response to proximity, making the water behave differently when observed versus ignored.

What it taught me

Flow proved that a short timeline is no excuse for a weak concept. By committing early to a strong idea with real emotional resonance, the three weeks felt purposeful rather than rushed.

The project also deepened my understanding of interaction — how a physical object can feel responsive and alive without a screen or interface. The water's behaviour communicates everything.
